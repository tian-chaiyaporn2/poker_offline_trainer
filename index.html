🔰 New to poker? Start here (30 seconds)

You and one opponent each get 2 secret cards (only you see yours). Then 5 shared cards everyone can use are dealt in stages: 3 at once, then a 4th, then a 5th. You make your best five-card hand from your 2 cards plus the shared ones.

At each stage you choose: Check (pass, bet nothing), Bet (put chips in), Call (match a bet), Raise (bet even more), or Fold (give up the hand). The chips already in the middle are the pot — that's what you're playing for.

One player acts first, the other acts last. Acting last is an advantage — you see what your opponent does before deciding. The trainer tells you which you are each hand.

Your job: pick the action a strong player would — graded instantly, told why.     Poker terms — tap to learn the lingo
    The board / shared cards
    The cards in the middle everyone can use — up to 5 of them.
    Flop, turn, river
    The stages the shared cards arrive in: the flop is the first 3, the turn is the 4th, the river is the 5th (last).
    Your hand (hole cards)
    Your 2 secret cards that only you can see.
    Pot
    The chips already in the middle — what you're playing to win.
    Blinds (small / big)
    Forced bets two players post before the cards, so there's always something to play for.
    Check · Bet · Call · Raise · Fold
    Pass (no bet) · put chips in · match a bet · bet even more · give up the hand.
    Position — Button (BTN), Big Blind (BB), Small Blind (SB)
    Where you sit. After the flop, the player in position (usually the Button) acts last — an advantage; out of position acts first. In blind-vs-blind, the BB is in position.
    The seats, first to last — UTG, Hijack (HJ), Cutoff (CO), Button (BTN), then the blinds (SB, BB)
    UTG acts first pre-flop (tightest, most players still behind); the Button acts last (widest). "The Hijack" and "the Cutoff" are the two seats just before the Button.
    In / out of position
    Whether you act last (in position) or first (out of position) on each street.
    Open (raise first-in / RFI)
    The first player to enter the pot by raising, pre-flop, when everyone before folded.
    3-bet / 4-bet
    A re-raise. The blind's raise is the "1st bet"; the open is the 2nd; re-raising the open is the 3-bet; re-raising that is the 4-bet.
    C-bet (continuation bet)
    Betting the flop after you were the one who raised before it.
    Value bet
    Betting a strong hand to get called by weaker ones.
    Bluff / semi-bluff
    Betting a weak hand to make better hands fold. A semi-bluff is a draw that can still improve.
    Bluff-catch
    Calling with a medium hand mainly to beat the times they're bluffing.
    Pot control
    Checking a decent-but-not-great hand to keep the pot small.
    Trap
    Checking a very strong hand to let opponents catch up or bluff into you.
    EV (expected value)
    Your average profit from a play, measured in big blinds (bb).
    Range
    All the different hands a player could have in this exact spot.
    Pot odds
    The price you're getting to call, compared with the size of the pot.
